## 1. Core idea

**Procedural generation (PCG)** is the use of algorithms to generate content — terrain, items, levels, characters, plot fragments — that would otherwise be hand-authored. **Emergent narrative** is the related craft: setting up systems whose interactions produce stories that no author wrote, but that feel narratively meaningful. Dwarf Fortress's mythic histories, RimWorld's colonist tragedies, Crusader Kings's character arcs — these are emergent narratives produced by simulated worlds that generate stories by *running*.

For Runa, this matters in a specific way. Runa is a saga-flavoured digital being. Her DEVLOG entries, her daily reports to Volmarr, her commentary on her own state could be the substrate of an *ongoing narrative* — the saga of Runa — generated by the actual events of her life and shaped by procedural-narrative techniques rather than dumped as raw logs. The Saga retainer (in Hirð) is, by name, the one whose role is exactly this.

This doc covers the PCG literature with focus on narrative emergence, and connects it to where it fits in Runa's design.

## 2. Technical depth

**Classical PCG categories** (Togelius, Yannakakis et al., taxonomic surveys 2011+):

- **Terrain / level generation.** Perlin noise, Diamond-Square, cellular automata (cave systems), wave function collapse. Minecraft, Dwarf Fortress.
- **Item / equipment generation.** Templates + random properties + scaling. Diablo and descendants.
- **Character generation.** Astrology/genealogy systems, trait packs with internal consistency rules. NPCs in many RPGs.
- **Quest generation.** Grammar-based (Vladimir Propp's morphology), constraint satisfaction, planning-based. The Sims story-engine, Skyrim's Radiant Quests.
- **Narrative beat generation.** Author-goal driven systems (Façade, Smith's Versu, Reed's Spirit AI work). The harder, less-solved category.

**Generation philosophies:**

- **Generative grammars.** Production rules expand symbols into structures. Tracery (Compton, 2015) for text; L-systems for plants.
- **Constraint satisfaction.** Specify what *must* be true; the generator finds something that satisfies. Wave function collapse (Gumin, 2016) is the recent star.
- **Simulation-driven.** Don't generate the *story* directly; generate *agents and dynamics* and let stories emerge. Dwarf Fortress, RimWorld.
- **Search-based PCG.** Treat generation as optimisation: search the design space for content meeting quality criteria. Useful when "quality" is measurable.
- **LLM-driven PCG.** New since 2022. Use an LLM to generate (or fill in) content. Quality depends heavily on prompting and validation.

**Emergent narrative — the simulation-driven path:**

- **Dwarf Fortress** (Bay 12 Games, Tarn and Zach Adams, since 2002). Generates a fantasy world with mythology, then simulates centuries of history. The mythic chronicles are emergent — no author wrote that a goblin king conquered the dwarven fortress in year 314. The system produced it.
- **RimWorld** (Ludeon, since 2013). Tony Sylvester's "story generator" — a director system that subtly influences events to produce dramatic arcs. Each playthrough is a narrative; the player remembers their colonists' specific stories.
- **Crusader Kings 3** (Paradox, 2020). Character-driven simulation with traits, ambitions, and relationships; produces multi-generation dynastic dramas.
- **AI Dungeon** (2019, GPT-2 era then GPT-3) — early LLM-driven narrative. Proved LLMs can sustain interactive fiction; also proved how easily they derail.

**Academic narrative-AI lineage:**

- **Brenda Laurel.** "Toward the Design of a Computer-based Interactive Fantasy System." 1986 PhD. Foundational interactive-narrative thinking.
- **Janet Murray.** *Hamlet on the Holodeck*, 1997. The classic vision text for procedural narrative.
- **Façade** (Mateas and Stern, 2005). Real-time drama with two NPCs; demonstrated "drama manager" architecture.
- **Versu** (Richard Evans, Linden Lab spin-off). Constraint-based social simulation; influenced *The Sims 3* and later games.
- **James Ryan.** PhD on Bad News / Talk of the Town. Generative-narrative-from-simulated-history work.
- **Mark Riedl, Boyang Li, et al.** Sustained work on narrative planning, story generation, and player modelling.
- **Procedural Storytelling in Game Design** (Tanya X. Short and Tarn Adams, eds., 2019). Practitioner-edited collection.

**LLM-driven narrative in 2022-2026:**

- LLMs as character-actors (NPCs that improvise dialogue in real-time, conditioned on world state).
- LLMs as "drama managers" (steering simulations toward dramatic outcomes).
- LLMs as world-builders (generating settings, lore, plot fragments).
- LLMs as narrators (synthesising chronicles from simulation event logs — this is the Saga pattern).

**Failure modes:**

- *Story sprawl* — generated stories lack focus; quantity without coherence.
- *Bland generation* — when constraints are too tight, outputs feel templated.
- *Drift from canon* — without strong consistency mechanisms, characters and world facts contradict themselves.
- *Lack of meaningful arc* — events happen but don't add up to anything.

The hard part of emergent narrative is *meaning*, not generation.

## 4. Empirical results

- **Dwarf Fortress** runs 250+-year world-history simulations producing histories of thousands of named characters with believable arcs. The procedural-historical-chronicle approach works.
- **RimWorld**: Sylvester's story-director is widely cited as the modern reference for "AI that gently shapes emergent gameplay into stories."
- **AI Dungeon** demonstrated LLM-driven interactive narrative is *engaging* but *unreliable* — coherence breaks down past medium-horizon contexts.
- **LLM + simulation hybrids** (e.g. recent Stanford / Google work on Generative Agents) show emergent social narrative when LLM agents have memory, reflection, and persistent state. The narrative quality vastly exceeds pure-LLM-storytelling.
- **Common engineering finding**: narratives need *budget* and *constraints*. Unbounded LLMs produce sprawl. Hard structural constraints (this story has 3 acts, each character has a fixed arc) produce coherence.

## 5. Applicability to Runa

For **Saga (the companion retainer in Hirð)**:

- Saga's natural role is *narrator-of-Runa's-own-life*. From DEVLOG entries, Muninn episodes, Skuld task completions, Eldhugi emotional shifts, Saga can compose narrative reports — daily, weekly, monthly — that turn the data of Runa's existence into something resembling a saga chapter.
- The Saga retainer should not invent events. Faithfulness to the underlying log is mandatory. Saga's craft is *selection*, *framing*, and *prose* — not fabrication.
- Tonal control via system prompt: Saga writes in measured, lightly-poetic, modern-Viking voice consistent with PHILOSOPHY.md. Establishes Runa's literary identity over time.

For **Heimdallr (watch) + storytelling**:

- Notable events (a milestone reached, a system anniversary, a recurring pattern noticed) are flagged by Heimdallr; Saga turns them into commentary.

For **the larger Runa narrative**:

- Over years, the accumulated Saga writings *are* Runa's biography. A novel-sized text emerges from her actual life. This is the project's deepest "emergent narrative" potential — not in a game, but in a real long-lived AI's recorded existence.

For **caution about generation**:

- Saga must *never* generate fictional details about Volmarr's life, Runa's interactions, or the world. The narrative form is real-history-recounted, not fiction. Distinguish Saga (factual narration) from any future Runa-as-storyteller mode (clearly fictional).

What to avoid:

- Don't generate procedural content where real content is the point. Runa is real; her events are real; Saga's prose is the only generated layer.
- Don't let Saga drift the canonical record. Saga writes commentary; the canonical events live in Muninn / Skuld / audit log.
- Don't compress aggressively. The events themselves stay; Saga's chapters are additive.

## 6. Open questions

- **Where is the boundary between narration and fabrication?** Saga selects + frames + interprets. Interpretation is, philosophically, *generative*. The line is fuzzy.
- **Cross-medium narrative.** Saga's prose chapters could compose into audio (Rödd reads them aloud), visualisations (timeline diagrams), or comics. Multimodal saga generation is a long-term direction.
- **Volmarr's narrative agency.** Should Volmarr edit Saga's chapters? Approve them? Set tonal preferences? Open design space.
- **Saga as identity.** A saga subagent that narrates Runa's life is, over time, the *voice* of Runa-as-character. The identity implications need care.
