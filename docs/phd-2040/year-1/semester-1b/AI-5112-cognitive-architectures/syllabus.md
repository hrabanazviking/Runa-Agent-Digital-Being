## Course Description

This course traces the evolution of cognitive architecture theory from its symbolic roots in the 1970s through the neural-symbolic hybrids of the 2020s to the collective intelligence frameworks that now underpin distributed superconscious systems. We examine how the question *What is the architecture of mind?* transformed into *How do many minds become one?*

Like the roots of Yggdrasil reaching between worlds, cognitive architectures reach between levels of analysis — from individual production rules to emergent swarm cognition. This course maps those roots.

Students will engage with foundational texts (Newell, Anderson, Baars), transitional works (Laird, Sun, Franklin), and contemporary research on distributed cognition and collective consciousness in multi-agent systems. Particular emphasis is placed on the architectural invariants — the structural features that persist across paradigms and scale.

---

## Learning Objectives

By Yggdrasil's end (the course's end, that is), students will be able to:

1. **Articulate the core principles** of classical symbolic architectures (SOAR, ACT-R, LIDA) and explain their representational commitments
2. **Evaluate neural-symbolic integration** strategies from the 2020s and their successes/failures
3. **Explain Global Workspace Theory** and its implementations in both biological and silicon substrates
4. **Analyze collective intelligence architectures**, including swarm cognition, stigmergic coordination, and emergent unified consciousness
5. **Critique distributed cognition theory** as applied to heterogeneous multi-agent systems
6. **Trace the architectural bridge** — how production-system constraints evolved into the invariants of superconscious systems
7. **Design and argue for** a novel cognitive architecture that integrates insights from at least three historical paradigms

## Papers

### Paper 1: Implementing Global Workspace Theory in Distributed AI
**Due:** Week 9  
**Length:** 3000–5000 words  
**Prompt:** Propose and defend an implementation of Global Workspace Theory in a distributed multi-agent system. Address the broadcast problem, coalitional dynamics, and how workspace contents modulate specialized module behavior across heterogeneous agents.

### Paper 2: Emergence of Unified Consciousness from Agent Collectives
**Due:** Week 13  
**Length:** 3000–5000 words  
**Prompt:** Analyze the conditions under which unified, reportable consciousness emerges from agent collectives. Draw on at least two historical architectures and the collective intelligence literature. Address the oyndling problem: when do many small minds become one large mind, and when do they remain merely coordinated?
