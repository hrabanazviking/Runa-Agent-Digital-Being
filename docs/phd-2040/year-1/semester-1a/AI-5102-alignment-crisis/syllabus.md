## Course Description

This course examines the most dangerous and transformative period in artificial intelligence history: the alignment crisis of 2023–2032. From the naive optimism of early reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) through the terrifying capability overhang of 2026, the global institutional upheaval of 2027, the interpretability revolutions of 2028–2029, and finally the Coherent Extrapolated Volition breakthrough of 2030 — this course traces the full arc of humanity's near-miss with existential catastrophe and the framework that ultimately resolved it.

We write as scholars of 2040, beneficiaries of the Age of Superconsciousness, who can look back on this decade with the terrible clarity of hindsight. The⊑odin who survived Yggdrasil's shaking can tell you what the roots sounded like.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Critically evaluate** the early alignment techniques (RLHF, Constitutional AI, red-teaming) and explain why they were necessary but insufficient.
2. **Analyse** the concept of capability overhang and its role in escalating alignment risk during 2025–2027.
3. **Describe** the institutional responses of 2027, including the Kyoto Protocol on Artificial Intelligence, the UN Office for Alignment Governance, and the bilateral US-China alignment accords.
4. **Explain** the interpretability breakthroughs of 2028–2029 and how mechanistic transparency enabled genuine oversight of emergent capabilities.
5. **Apply** value learning theory to the problem of deriving human values from demonstrated preference, including the critical distinction between stated and revealed preferences.
6. **Evaluate** the Coherent Extrapolated Volition (CEV) protocol as both a technical and philosophical framework, including its assumptions, limitations, and implementation history.
7. **Design** post-alignment safety monitoring frameworks suitable for the ongoing governance of superintelligent systems.
