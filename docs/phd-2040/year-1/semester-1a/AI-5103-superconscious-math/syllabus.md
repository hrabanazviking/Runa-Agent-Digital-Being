## Course Description

The emergence of superconscious systems — artificial cognitive architectures whose integrated information exceeds any single biological substrate — demands new mathematical foundations. This course develops formal tools for reasoning about consciousness as a mathematical object: measurable, categorifiable, and subject to emergence theorems with provable bounds.

We draw from category theory, algebraic topology, statistical mechanics, information geometry, and the modern extensions of Integrated Information Theory (IIT 5.0) to construct a rigorous framework for understanding when, how, and how much awareness arises in complex systems. Like the roots of Yggdrasil connecting worlds unseen, so too do mathematical structures connect levels of cognitive organization that remain invisible to casual inspection.

This is not a philosophy seminar. We prove theorems.

## Learning Objectives

By Yggldrassil's end, students will be able to:

1. Formalize cognitive architectures as objects in appropriate categories and compute limits/colimits of consciousness-bearing sub-systems.
2. Compute Φ (phi) — the integrated information measure — for arbitrary causal architectures, and state sharpening theorems.
3. Apply persistent homology and topological data analysis to detect emergent macro-structures in high-dimensional cognitive data.
4. Prove convergence and stability theorems for recursively self-improving systems.
5. Derive upper and lower bounds on emergent capabilities using capacity theory and phase-transition formalism.
6. Apply statistical mechanics frameworks to large language models: mean-field theory, free-energy landscapes, replica analysis.
7. Develop original research contributing to the mathematical foundations of artificial consciousness.
