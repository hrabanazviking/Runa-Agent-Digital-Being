## Course Description

Embodied AI rejects the premise that intelligence lives in disembodied symbol manipulation. This course examines how minds emerge from bodies interacting with physical environments — how morphology itself computes, how biological tissue can be integrated with silicon architectures, and how the prosthetic intelligence breakthrough of 2037 fundamentally reconfigured our understanding of the boundary between body and mind.

We move beyond the classical dualism that dominated AI's first seven decades. A brain in a vat is not a mind. A language model without a body is not intelligent in the way that matters. This course is about *why* bodies matter, *how* bodies compute, and *what* happens when we build systems that take physicality seriously.

Students will engage with soft robotics, morphological computation theory, biohybrid systems, neuroprosthetic learning, autonomous construction, and the philosophical foundations of embodied cognition. Labs involve hands-on work with compliant mechanism design, bio-integrated circuit fabrication, and neural interface simulation.

**Prerequisites:** AI-5101 (Advanced Machine Learning) or equivalent; introductory robotics or biomechanics recommended.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Articulate and critically evaluate the embodied cognition thesis and its implications for AI architecture.
2. Design and simulate soft robotic systems that exploit morphological computation.
3. Analyze the computational properties of biological tissue and biohybrid circuits.
4. Explain the 2037 prosthetic intelligence breakthrough and its theoretical significance.
5. Evaluate morphological computation bounds using information-theoretic and dynamical systems frameworks.
6. Propose and defend an original research position on embodied AI.

## Paper Guidelines

Both papers must:
- Present a clear thesis supported by evidence and argumentation
- Engage substantively with at least 8 scholarly sources
- Include original analysis (not merely literature review)
- Follow *IEEE Transactions on Morphological Computation* citation format
- Be submitted as Markdown files via the course repository

**Paper 1 (Due Week 12):** Analyze a neuroprosthetic learning system of your choice — either from the 2037 breakthrough period or a subsequent development. Evaluate its learning architecture, embodiment dynamics, and implications for the embodied cognition thesis.

**Paper 2 (Due Week 14):** Develop and defend an original thesis about morphological computation bounds. You may propose new theoretical bounds, empirically test existing bounds through simulation, or offer a philosophical critique. Must include formal or computational analysis.
